Kick-off Call Script

[CLIENT NAME] · [COMPANY] · ~40 min
1
Opening
2 min
"Hey [CLIENT], good to finally kick this off. Today is mainly about alignment — making sure we both know exactly what success looks like, how the pilot works, and who does what. I'll walk you through the plan quickly, then ask a few questions so I can set everything up properly."
  • Share screen → open kickoff-2026-05-28.html in browser
2
Walk through the strategy
5–7 min
"I have roughly 1,000 food industry companies across [PRIMARY MARKETS] — with the broader region it's closer to 2,000 relevant contacts. I'll go through the market twice during the pilot.

At the same time I'm continuously monitoring for signals — companies opening new facilities, leadership changes, acquisitions, hiring spikes. When something strong comes up I put together a quick brief with the right contact, context, and suggested angle — and route it to you so you can act on it fast.

The goal is 2 qualified introductions. Once the conversation happens, the recruiting process is on your side."
3
His current process Key question
5–7 min
"Before I ask about the market scope — can you walk me through how you currently find new clients? What does that process look like for you right now?"
  • Do you call prospects directly, or mostly email and LinkedIn?
  • Are you using Sales Navigator actively?
  • What's been working well? What hasn't?
Why this matters: if he calls regularly → signal cards work perfectly, he just executes. If he doesn't → you need to own more of the first-touch outreach yourself.
"One thing I know works well — instead of writing companies 'I know a recruiter', I write them 'I have a specific candidate who could be relevant for you.' Much better response rate. Do you have a pool of candidates you're currently working with — even just anonymised profiles I can use as the hook? I'm not sharing names, just something like '12 years food manufacturing, supply chain, open to [PRIMARY MARKETS].'"
  • Do you have active candidates you're representing right now?
  • Can you share anonymised profiles — background, specialisation, geography?
  • How often does this list update?
If yes: this changes the outreach angle completely — candidate-led is stronger than recruiter-led. Build a simple shared doc where he drops new profiles as they come in.
↓
Time vs effectiveness — his preference
3 min
"One thing I want to understand — how much time per week can you realistically put into this? Because it changes how I structure things.

For signal opportunities, there are two ways. I can reach out myself — but I don't speak French or German, I'm not the recruiter, so the hit rate will be lower. Or you make the calls — you speak their language, you know the industry, the conversion is going to be much higher. The market is small, so that difference matters.

Realistically we're talking maybe 3–5 signal calls per week, not more. So — what's more important to you: saving your time, or maximising the result in absolute terms?"
  • If he says time → you own more of the outreach, lower effectiveness, adjust expectations
  • If he says results → signal cards go to him, he calls, you track outcomes
  • Note from first call: [CAPACITY NOTE]
4
Market scope — expand, not narrow
5 min
"Core focus is [PRIMARY MARKETS] — around 550 food industry companies. I also have the broader region: [EXPANSION MARKETS] — that's another ~270 food companies, roughly +50% more volume. Want to include that as a secondary layer, or stay focused on DE and FR for now?"
  • NL + BE + LU + CH + AT — include as secondary scope?
  • I also have ~200 food-adjacent companies: large retailers like [RETAILER EXAMPLES], food ingredient suppliers, food packaging. Relevant or stay pure food manufacturers?
  • Any segments to exclude — farming, food service?
  • Any companies you already have active relationships with that I should skip?
5
Qualified intro — align clearly Critical
3–5 min
"Just want to make sure we're on the same page on what counts as a qualified intro. My definition: right company, right country, the person who makes hiring decisions said yes to a conversation. Does that sound fair to you?"
  • If I send you a signal card and you call them — and the conversation leads somewhere — that counts as a qualified intro on my side. Fair?
Important: get explicit verbal confirmation on this. This protects you if he later questions results.
6
Operational workflow
5 min
  • Do you use Slack? If yes — I'll set up a shared channel for signal updates and quick decisions. If not — Google Sheet + WhatsApp works fine.
  • When someone from the outreach replies "yes, I'm interested" and I loop you in — how fast can you respond? Ideally within 24 hours. If it sits for 3 days the conversation goes cold. Is that realistic for you?
  • When I send you a lead: do you prefer a full draft ready to send, or a short brief so you can write it yourself?
7
Reality check
3 min
"Last thing — what's your biggest concern about this working? Anything you think could be a bottleneck?"
  • Listen carefully — this often reveals hidden expectations or constraints
  • Common answers: "I'm very busy", "I've tried this before", "quality of contacts matters most"
8
Next steps
2 min
"Perfect. So: I handle research, outreach, signals, and filtering leads. You step in for the actual conversations. Goal is 2 qualified intros during the pilot.

From my side: target list is ready, first emails go out next week, signals start running in parallel. You'll hear from me in a few days."
