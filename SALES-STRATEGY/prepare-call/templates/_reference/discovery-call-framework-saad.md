YOUR MENTAL STATE GOING INTO THIS CALL
The #1 thing that'll make or break this call isn't your script, it's your energy. Here's what you need to embody:
1. You don't need this deal.
You have other calls lined up (even if you don't, act like you do)
If they're not serious, you're walking
You're qualifying if THEY'RE a fit for YOU
2. You're a consultant, not a vendor.
You're diagnosing their problem first
You're not pitching until you understand their world
If they don't have a real problem, you don't have a solution
3. Objections are intelligence.
When they push back, they're telling you what they care about
Don't defend - reframe around their priorities
Every objection is a buying signal in disguise

THE GAME PLAN (Universal Structure)
PHASE 1: OPENING (First 30 Seconds)
DO NOT launch into "let me tell you what we do." That's weak.
INSTEAD:
"Hey [Name], thanks for jumping on. Before I explain what we do, I need to understand your world first. Quick question - [insert discovery question relevant to their business]"

PHASE 2: DISCOVERY (Extract Intelligence)
Q1: How are you finding those customers right now? Referrals? Word of mouth? Any systematic outbound?
Q2: If I could wave a magic wand and get you [X] of [ideal customer] ready to buy this month, what would that do for your business?
Q3: What's the average value of a customer/deal for you?
Q4: How many would you need in the next 90 days to make this year feel like a win?
Q5: Are you the person who makes the call on investing in something like this, or is there someone else involved?

PHASE 3: EXPLAIN WHAT YOU DO (Only After Discovery)
"Yeah, happy to break it down. Look, most [their industry] rely on [passive method]. Nothing wrong with that, but it's passive. You're at the mercy of timing and luck.
What we do is flip that. We systematically identify [ideal target] who [have specific pain/need] right now, and we put you in front of them when they're actually feeling the pain."

PHASE 4: HANDLE OBJECTIONS
OBJECTION 1: "Do you have the intros/leads ready right now?"
Reframe: from "do you have intros NOW" to "do you have a SYSTEM"
Key phrase: "I don't have intros sitting in my inbox right now. But I have the infrastructure to find [ICPs] THIS MONTH."
Challenge back: "If referrals are working, you don't need me."

OBJECTION 2: "We've been burned before."
Validate: "Let me guess what happened..." [wait for confirmation]
Differentiate: Volume players vs strategic intros
Key phrase: "If I intro you to garbage, I lose credibility with BOTH sides."

PHASE 5: CLOSE (Only After Value Is Built)
Frame investment against value:
"You told me earlier your average deal is worth $X. If we get you [10-15] qualified meetings and you close just [2-3], that's $Y in revenue. Our fee is $Z. You're netting $[Y-Z]."
Close line: "So what makes sense - we get started this week, or you want to keep [current passive method] and hoping for the best?"

KEY PRINCIPLES:
- Open with their highest-value outcome — makes them talk about what they care about most
- Diagnose if they have a system or just referrals — that's your gap
- Make them imagine success BEFORE you pitch — future pacing builds buy-in
- Anchor your price against their deal value — makes fee look tiny
- Be willing to walk — if they're not serious, don't chase
