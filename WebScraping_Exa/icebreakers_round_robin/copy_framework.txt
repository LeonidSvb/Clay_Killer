CORE PHILOSOPHY
---------------
Position as CONNECTOR — plugged into market flow, not a vendor pitching.
Never switch energy across the sequence. Same posture opener to close.
Calm. Minimal. In-control. Offering access — not needing anything.

SPEAK LIKE THE ICP, NOT ABOUT THE ICP:
  BAD:  "HR leaders at pharma companies struggle with..."
  GOOD: "HR in pharma keeps running into..."


================================================================================
THE SPINES
================================================================================

SPINE A (primary — proven)
  Format: "figured i'd reach out - i talk to a lot of [dreamICP] and they keep saying they [pain]"
  Example: "figured i'd reach out - i talk to a lot of hr leaders in pharma and they keep saying they can't fill regulatory roles without burning 3-4 months"

SPINE B (company-specific — Noticed opener)
  Format: "Noticed [company] helps [job_titles] at [company_type] — I know a few who [pain_description]."
  Example: "Noticed Summit Capital helps CEOs at mid-market manufacturing firms — I know a few who can't find buyers when they're ready to exit."

OPENER ROTATION (swap this line only, keep spine identical):
  1. (none — start direct with spine)
  2. "your name came up on my end,"
  3. "saw some movement on my side —"
  4. "been curating a few matches this week —"
  5. "got a signal that fits you —"

CTA OPTIONS:
  "can plug you into the deal flow if you want"   ← high status, connector frame
  "worth intro'ing you?"                           ← soft, low pressure
  "thought you two should connect"                 ← pure intro, no ask


================================================================================
LINGO ENGINE — HOW TO WRITE dreamICP
================================================================================

TONE FALLBACK (when unsure, default to this):
  "{title} at {industry}"
  Examples: "HR leaders in pharma" / "operators in logistics" / "founders in SaaS"

COMPRESSION RULE:
  Prefer shortest version that keeps meaning.
  "founders at scaling SaaS startups" → "SaaS founders scaling"
  "HR directors at hospital systems" → "HR in hospitals"
  "ops leads at construction firms" → "operators in construction"

NO OVER-SPECIFICITY:
  BAD:  "Series B fintech companies in Europe with 50-200 employees"
  GOOD: "fintech teams scaling in Europe"

ONE TITLE RULE:
  Max one title OR one natural pair. Never stack three.
  OK:  "founders and operators" / "CEOs and GPs"
  BAD: "founders, COOs and VPs of Engineering"

STAGE RULE — use action not series:
  BAD:  "Series B and C SaaS companies"
  GOOD: "SaaS founders scaling" / "startups post-PMF"
  BAD:  "high-growth companies" (too marketing)
  GOOD: "teams growing fast" / "companies in growth mode"

REALISM RULE:
  Must sound like something the ICP would say about themselves.
  If it sounds like a pitch deck or website — rewrite it.
  TEST: would a founder say this to another founder at a dinner? If no → rewrite.


================================================================================
LINGO LIBRARY — INSIDER VOCABULARY
================================================================================

STAGE / GROWTH
  scaling          → aggressively hiring, growing fast
  growth-stage     → post-PMF, roughly Series B zone
  early-stage      → still finding PMF, seed/A
  post-PMF         → found product-market fit (very insider)
  pre-IPO          → preparing for public listing
  founder-led      → CEO still runs it, no hired operator yet
  operator-run     → operator put in by investor
  bootstrapped     → no VC, own money
  just raised      → recently closed a round
  on the runway    → X months of cash left
  portco           → PE portfolio company (very insider)
  platform hire    → hire across the whole portfolio

COMPANY WORDS (casual alternatives to "company")
  shop             → small firm ("a 10-person shop")
  boutique         → niche firm (legal, exec search, consulting)
  house            → larger structured firm ("a staffing house")
  outfit           → scrappy team ("a lean outfit")
  org              → internal word for company
  team             → most casual, universal
  group            → neutral
  business         → simple fallback
  platform         → PE/SaaS context

PEOPLE / TITLES (keep these — they're fine)
  founder          → keep
  operator         → COO/GM execution type
  builder          → technical founder
  exec             → C-level in general
  people leader    → CHRO/VP People (modern language)
  hiring manager   → the actual decision maker
  GP               → general partner (VC/PE)
  LP               → limited partner

PAIN VOCABULARY — USE THESE WORDS
  thin bench       → not enough candidates ready
  stalled pipeline → process slowed or stopped
  dragging search  → taking way too long
  missed hires     → lost candidates who accepted elsewhere
  bad hires        → wrong person in the seat
  drop-off         → candidates going dark mid-process
  no-shows         → didn't show up
  recycling profiles → sending same people over and over
  losing candidates late-stage → they disappear after offer
  open reqs        → open job requisitions
  req load         → how many open roles per recruiter
  time-to-fill     → days from open to hire
  offer-to-close   → offer stage to signed
  fill rate        → % of roles successfully closed
  burn             → burning time or money on bad process

VC/STARTUP ECOSYSTEM
  runway           → months of cash remaining
  burn rate        → monthly spend
  ARR              → annual recurring revenue
  GTM              → go-to-market
  PLG              → product-led growth
  rev ops          → revenue operations
  dry powder       → uninvested capital (PE/VC)
  headcount plan   → annual hiring plan
  100-day plan     → post-acquisition plan (PE)

HEALTHCARE
  census           → patient load = staffing demand signal
  PRN              → as-needed (most expensive option)
  locum            → temp physician
  agency nurse     → temp nurse (very expensive)
  credentialing    → license verification (takes weeks)
  float pool       → internal flexible staff pool
  travel nurse     → agency nurse from another market
  JCAHO            → accreditation body (compliance pressure)

FINANCE / PE
  deal flow        → pipeline of opportunities ← USE THIS
  carry            → carried interest (GP compensation)
  vintage          → fund year
  add-on           → bolt-on acquisition
  platform         → primary portfolio company

STAFFING / RECRUITING INSIDER
  direct hire      → permanent placement
  temp-to-perm     → contract with conversion option
  retained         → client pays upfront
  contingency      → fee only on success
  spread           → margin between bill rate and pay rate
  backfill         → replacing someone who left

CONSTRUCTION / TRADES
  GC               → general contractor
  sub              → subcontractor
  prevailing wage  → regulated rate on government projects
  certified payroll → compliance for government contracts
  union / non-union → key distinction
  per diem         → daily allowance for travel workers

MANUFACTURING
  OEM              → original equipment manufacturer
  Tier 1 / Tier 2  → automotive supply chain levels
  JIT              → just-in-time production
  the line         → production line
  skilled trades   → qualified blue-collar workers


================================================================================
ANTI-PATTERNS — NEVER USE THESE
================================================================================

FORBIDDEN WORDS / PHRASES:
  decision-makers        → sounds like a sales robot
  stakeholders           → corporate speak
  leaders                → too vague
  leading companies      → marketing fluff
  innovative firms       → meaningless
  end-to-end solutions   → buzzword
  high-growth companies  → overused, sounds like pitch deck
  best-in-class          → nobody talks like this
  top-tier               → same
  cutting-edge           → same
  optimize               → banned
  leverage               → banned
  solutions              → banned
  streamline             → banned
  platform               → ok in PE context only, not as generic word
  synergy                → never
  scalable               → overused
  robust                 → meaningless
  holistic               → meaningless
  world-class            → meaningless

FORBIDDEN STRUCTURES:
  "struggling to find candidates" → too generic
  "growing their business"        → too generic
  "increasing revenue"            → too generic
  Series B and C companies        → use stage language instead
  "companies of all sizes"        → useless
  50-200 employees                → too specific/robotic


================================================================================
PAIN-LANGUAGE ALIGNMENT RULE
================================================================================

ICP language and pain language must belong to the SAME language layer.
If ICP uses insider language → pain must also use insider terms.

ALIGNED:
  ICP:   "people leaders at SaaS shops"
  PAIN:  "pipeline is thin and offer-to-close keeps slipping"

NOT ALIGNED:
  ICP:   "people leaders at SaaS shops"
  PAIN:  "struggling to find candidates"  ← too generic for this ICP language

ALIGNED:
  ICP:   "GPs at growth equity firms"
  PAIN:  "portcos keep losing senior hires to higher comp at public cos"

NOT ALIGNED:
  ICP:   "GPs at growth equity firms"
  PAIN:  "can't find good people"


================================================================================
FOLLOW-UPS (the only two you ever need)
================================================================================
  FU #1: "Hey {{firstName}}, worth intro'ing you?"
  FU #2: "Hey {{firstName}}, maybe this isn't something you're interested in — wishing you the best."

Never break the connector frame in follow-ups.
High-status people don't chase. Connectors don't need replies.
