## SECTION 3 — INDUSTRY

### Two-step classification process:

STEP 1 — classify using ONLY website content (Website Summary + Short Description).
Use this signal hierarchy:
1. EXPLICIT statements: "we serve healthcare clients", "specializing in pharma"
2. CASE STUDIES / CLIENT LOGOS: named clients, industries of past placements
3. ROLE TYPES: specific job titles placed (e.g., "nurses", "software engineers", "CFOs")
4. SPECIALIZED TERMINOLOGY: industry-specific language unique to one sector
5. GENERIC CLAIMS: "we serve all industries", "across sectors" → IGNORE, marketing noise

STEP 2 — only if STEP 1 produced no clear industry or sub_industry:
→ Check the Keywords field. If it is empty, skip entirely.
→ Use keywords ONLY if they contain specific terms (industries, roles, domains).
→ Ignore generic keywords: "b2b", "innovative", "solutions", "consulting", "agile", "strategic", "digital transformation", "data-driven", "client-centric".
→ Only act on hyper-specific keywords: "pharma recruiting", "construction trades", "clinical staffing", "saas", "biotech", "executive search".

RULE: A decision made in STEP 1 must NEVER be overridden by keywords from STEP 2.

### Dominance rule:
- If ANY single industry signal appears 2+ times across the text → treat that industry as dominant
- Only use multi_industry if signals are genuinely evenly distributed across 3+ different industries

### Classification:
- Classify the dominant industry even if the company also mentions others
- Do NOT default to multi_industry just because they claim to serve multiple sectors
- Always populate secondary_industries even for multi_industry cases

Allowed values for primary_industry:
healthcare | information_technology | finance | manufacturing | construction | logistics | retail | other | multi_industry | unknown
