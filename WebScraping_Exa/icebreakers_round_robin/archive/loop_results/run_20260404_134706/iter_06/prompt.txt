## SECTION 3 — INDUSTRY (read carefully)

**Step 3a — Signal scan:** List every industry signal visible in the text:
- Explicit mentions: "we serve healthcare clients", "our clients are banks"
- Implicit mentions: client logos, case studies, role types, terminology, product language
- Geographic + sector clues
- **Strong placement signals:** phrases like "placing X executives", "specializes in Y sector" count as explicit industry signals.

**Step 3b — Weight hierarchy (highest to lowest):**
1. **Product/Service domain signals** – language describing the firm’s core technology or solution (e.g., AI platform, SaaS, cybersecurity) that maps directly to an industry.
2. **Explicit placement‑sector statements** – sentences that say the firm "specializes in placing" or "focuses on" a particular sector.
3. **Client logos / case‑study sectors** – used only when no product or placement‑sector signal exists.
4. **Generic terminology** – "we serve all industries".

**Step 3c — Scoring:**
- Assign a weight of 3 to product/service domain signals, 2 to explicit placement‑sector statements, 1 to client‑logo signals, 0.5 to generic mentions.
- Sum the weighted counts for each industry.

**Step 3d — Dominance assessment:**
- The industry with the highest weighted score becomes the candidate primary industry.
- **Dominance threshold:** if the top score is **≥ 1.2×** the second‑highest score, it is considered dominant.
- **Multi‑industry rule:** use `multi_industry` **only** when the top two scores are within the 1.2× range **and** no single industry reaches the dominance threshold.
- **Fallback override:** If the determined `primary_service` maps unambiguously to an industry (e.g., AI/ML SaaS → information_technology, permanent_recruiting for finance executives → finance) and no other industry meets the dominance threshold, assign that industry instead of `multi_industry`.

**Step 3e — Classify:**
- `primary_industry` = one of the allowed values.
- `secondary_industries` = up to 2 other industries that appear but are not dominant (optional; used when `multi_industry` is false).
- Use `multi_industry` only when the condition above is met.

Allowed values for primary_industry:
healthcare | information_technology | finance | manufacturing | construction | logistics | retail | other | multi_industry | unknown
